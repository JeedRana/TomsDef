Rules of Billiards:

    1.1 Player's Responsibility:
    It is the player's responsibility to be aware of all rules, regulations and schedules applying to competition.
    1.2 Lagging to Determine Order of Play:
    The lag is the first shot of the match and determines order of play. The player who wins the lag chooses who will shoot first.
    1.3 Player's Use of Equipment:
    The equipment must meet existing WPA equipment specifications. In general, players are not permitted to introduce novel equipment into the game.
    1.4 Spotting Balls:
    Balls are spotted (returned to play on the table) by placing them on the long string (long axis of the table) as close as possible to the foot spot and between the foot spot and the foot rail, without moving any interfering ball.
    1.5 Cue Ball in Hand:
    When the cue ball is in hand, the shooter may place the cue ball anywhere on the playing surface (see 8.1 Parts of the Table) and may continue to move the cue ball until he executes a shot. (See definition 8.2 Shot.)
    1.6 Standard Call Shot:
    In games in which the shooter is required to call shots, the intended ball and pocket must be indicated for each shot if they are not obvious.
    1.7 Balls Settling:
    A ball may settle slightly after it appears to have stopped, possibly due to slight imperfections in the ball or the table.
